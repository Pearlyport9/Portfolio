## Case Studies

### 1. Everiithing (Solo)

**One-line summary:** Nigeria's first fully managed, verification-first marketplace for home and business service providers, designed and built end-to-end, from concept through full-stack deployment.

**Assets (for agent):**
- HERO: Video (autoplay, muted, looped, no controls), place at top of case study page as full-width hero
- SCREENS: Dashboard screens below the video, show 3–4 key screens in this order: customer booking view, provider dashboard, admin panel
- Asset files: [TO FILL, add video filename and screen filenames when uploading to /assets/work/everiithing/]

**Problem:**
Finding a reliable artisan in Nigeria is a persistent everyday frustration. Customers face no reliable vetting (WhatsApp/Instagram are flooded with self-promoted, unverifiable providers), poor workmanship with no accountability, inflated and opaque pricing negotiated ad hoc, frequent no-shows, and zero formal recourse when things go wrong. On the other side, skilled artisans are invisible: buried under noise from unqualified competitors, with no way to build a verifiable professional identity, access consistent job leads, or grow a steady income pipeline. Both sides are stuck navigating a market with no trust infrastructure.

**Process:**
- Designed and built solo as a fully managed marketplace: the platform controls pricing, quality, and customer experience end-to-end to close the trust gap
- Built a 7-stage provider verification pipeline (document submission, NIN identity match, reference calls, guarantor submission, in-person interview, practical skills test, platform onboarding) as the core trust differentiator
- Designed flat-rate, transparent pricing across 7 service categories (plumbing, electrical, AC, generator/inverter, painting, deep cleaning, carpentry): no hidden fees, no negotiation
- Designed the full booking-to-payout flow: browse verified providers → instant booking → escrow payment → job completion confirmation → funds released to provider → rating/review
- Designed and built three connected surfaces: customer-facing booking app, provider dashboard (job notifications, earnings tracker), and an internal admin/ops panel (verification queue, dispute resolution, pricing management)
- MVP scoped deliberately: excluded subscription tiers, live chat, GPS tracking, and native mobile apps from v1 to ship a focused Lagos pilot first

**Outcome:**
Shipped and soft-launched to early testers. Everiithing is live and actively onboarding its first customers and verified service providers across Lagos.

**Live link:** https://everiithing-com.vercel.app/

### 4. ReceiptVault (Collaborative, capstone project)

**One-line summary:** A mobile expense tracking app built for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers, entering an uncontested gap in the local market.

**Assets (for agent):**
- HERO: Static screen, strongest app screen as full-width hero image
- SCREENS: 3–4 supporting screens showing key flows
- Asset files: [TO FILL, add screen filenames when uploading to /assets/work/receiptvault/]

**Problem:**
Most small business owners and freelancers in Nigeria track expenses through accounting software like QuickBooks, Sage, or Wave, or default to Excel spreadsheets, and the less organized resort to physical drawers, handwritten notes, or nothing at all. None of these offer a fast, mobile-first, real-time expense capture experience. Existing alternatives fall short for different reasons: Expensify is foreign-built, dollar-priced, and designed for teams rather than solo operators; Wave is free but not localized for Nigeria; Pearmonie is the closest local competitor but focuses on sales tracking rather than expense capture. No existing tool combines mobile expense capture, receipt photo storage, categorization, and tax-ready reporting for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers specifically: a gap made more urgent by Nigeria's new tax reforms, which now require businesses to maintain verifiable digital expense records to qualify for tax exemptions.

**Process:** Contributed to the market intelligence research phase: identifying the gap in the Nigerian expense tracking space and analysing competing tools. Designed the UI interface, translating research insights into clean, mobile-first screens for the core expense tracking experience.

**Outcome:**
Completed as a collaborative capstone project and shipped live. ReceiptVault is accessible and ready for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers to start tracking expenses digitally.
