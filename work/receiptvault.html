ReceiptVault

A mobile expense tracking app built for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers, entering an uncontested gap in the local market.

Hero image — TO FILL

Problem

Most small business owners and freelancers in Nigeria track expenses through accounting software like QuickBooks, Sage, or Wave, or default to Excel spreadsheets — and the less organized resort to physical drawers, handwritten notes, or nothing at all. None of these offer a fast, mobile-first, real-time expense capture experience. Existing alternatives fall short for different reasons: Expensify is foreign-built, dollar-priced, and designed for teams rather than solo operators; Wave is free but not localized for Nigeria; Pearmonie is the closest local competitor but focuses on sales tracking rather than expense capture. No existing tool combines mobile expense capture, receipt photo storage, categorization, and tax-ready reporting for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers specifically — a gap made more urgent by Nigeria's new tax reforms, which now require businesses to maintain verifiable digital expense records to qualify for tax exemptions.

Solution / Process

Contributed to the market intelligence research phase — identifying the gap in the Nigerian expense tracking space and analysing competing tools. Designed the UI interface, translating research insights into clean, mobile-first screens for the core expense tracking experience.

Outcome

Completed as a collaborative capstone project and shipped live. ReceiptVault is accessible and ready for Nigerian SMEs and freelancers to start tracking expenses digitally.
