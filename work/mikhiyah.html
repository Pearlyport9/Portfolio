Mikhiyah

Website for a mental wellness and community support organization.

Problem

Mikhiyah needed a warm, accessible website that reflected its mission of mental wellness and community support: a digital space that felt as safe and intentional as the organisation itself. Visitors typically arrive looking for mental health resources, mindfulness tools, or faith-based support during a vulnerable moment, so the site couldn't feel clinical, cold, or corporate. Tone and pacing needed to match the sensitivity of the subject matter. Without an existing site, the organisation had no central place to point people toward its resources, its on-the-ground community work (including its Guinea programme), or its mindfulness and spirituality offerings. Everything lived scattered across word-of-mouth and disconnected channels, or nowhere at all. The site also had to serve a broad audience with varying levels of digital comfort, which meant keeping navigation simple and reassuring rather than feature-heavy.

Solution / Process

  • Part of the collaborative team responsible for designing the UI interface, working from client-provided copy and brand direction.
  • Designed a card-based Resources grid (six cards across two rows of three) so visitors can quickly scan available support materials.
  • Built a split-screen Guinea section with three feature boxes to highlight the organisation's on-the-ground community work.
  • Structured a two-section layout separating Mindfulness and Faith/Spirituality content, keeping each pillar of support distinct and easy to navigate.
  • Designed the hero around the line "Tools for Healing, Hope, and Well-Being" to set a warm, welcoming tone from the first visit.
  • Kept the interface calm and accessible throughout, in line with the sensitivity a mental wellness audience needs.

Outcome

Delivered to the client and currently live at mikhiyah.org. The site serves the Mikhiyah community with accessible mental wellness resources, tools, and support content.
